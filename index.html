AI

As an early adopter of AI, I cannot just use AI without being highly critical of the output1.

I am a firm believer that AI is:

  1. underused
    • one of the most useful tools humanity has invented, hailed as “the great equalizer”
    • most people I have worked with are just catching up to what I used it for 2 years ago
  2. overused
    • prone to bubble bursting soon
    • thoughtlessly used for almost everything
    • potentially limiting human thinking and flourishing

Both points can be possible at the same time.

As such, I will explicitly state when I use AI. Transparency, and being explicit in your assumptions and intentions is something i value highly. You have my word - as much as it is worth - that what is produced here is my own.

I use AI for:

  • Quick brainstorming of anything
  • Teaching the structure of code and why and how it works
    • This is faster and much better formulated than documentation usually is

And not for generating quick bites of beautifully cohorent but frustratingly nonsensical AI text or code.
